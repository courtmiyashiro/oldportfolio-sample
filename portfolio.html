About

Courtney Miyashiro is a student attending James Madison University. She is currently pursuing a B.S in Media Arts and Design with a concentration in Interactive Design. She is also minoring in Computer Information Systems.

Courtney is interested in interactive design, user experience design, user interface design, and front-end web development. She is proficient in Adobe Creative Cloud, HTML, and CSS.

She is actively seeking experience within the design and technology field and hopes to be able to apply her creative, technical, and detail-oriented skills.

This portfolio website was hand-coded with multiple media queries by Courtney using HTML and the latest CSS layout spec, CSS Grid.

Visual Communication Design

SMAD 201 - "Your Story"

In her SMAD 201 class, Visual Communication Design, Courtney created a booklet based on her COVID-19 experience during the summer. Using Adobe InDesign and Photoshop, she was able to create a booklet that was based around specifically, her mental health experience during quarantine.

Web Design

SMAD 307 - Downtown Site

In her SMAD 307 class, Courtney hand-coded a website with information about Harrisonburg and JMU using HTML and CSS. Through this project, she created a Harrisonburg logo for the site. The site consisted of five pages all of which included downtown Harrisonburg and JMU information and photographs.

SMAD 307 - Retail Site

In her SMAD 307 class, Courtney hand-coded a ficticious retail website for a company called MOVE using HTML and CSS. The site supported multiple media queries. Through this project, she also created a logo, privacy policy, and was able to use already existing products. She chose to create an online athletic wear store which was inspired by one of her favorite stores, Lululemon.

SMAD 203 - CSS Zen Garden

In her SMAD 203 class, Courtney hand-coded a CSS Zen Garden site. This was one of the first websites Courtney learned how to code and an important one where she learned how to implement an aside bar.

User Experience Design

SMAD 203 & 317 - UX Research

In her SMAD 203 and 317 class (User Experience Design), Courtney was able gain knowledge on the basic user experience design fundamentals. This included conducting extensive user research which began with identifying a design problem, conducting interviews, storyboarding, creating personas, and designing prototypes/website.

SMAD 317 - UX Prototype

In her SMAD 317 class, Courtney and two other members in her class teamed up to create a mental health app called "Relax". Together, Courtney and her team collaborated together and conducted user research. Over the course of a whole semester, they were able to bring the app to life in the form of low and high-fidelity prototype that proved to be a success among several participants during usability testing.
